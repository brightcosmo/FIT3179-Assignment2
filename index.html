Deforestation

Global Data

Which countries have the most forest?

Forests account for a little over one-third (38%) of habitable land area on earth. This consists of around 26% of total (both habitable and uninhabitable) land area, which varies significantly between countries.

How have the most and least dense forests changed over time?

Countries with favorable conditions for tree growth, strong conservation policies, and low levels of deforestation tend to have higher tree densities. Conversely, countries with harsh climates, high levels of urbanization, and deforestation activities tend to have lower tree densities.

Forest Change for Major Countries

Countries with the lowest tree density such as Saudi Arabia and Bahrain have little to no disturbed forest or forest loss, as there are too little trees. Meanwhile, countries with higher density like Malaysia and Sweden have more of these factors.

Malaysia

Tree Cover Loss in Malaysia

Malaysia has experienced moderate levels of deforestation, with Johor, Pahang, and Negeri Sembilan losing out the most.

Plantations in Malaysia

Clearing land for plantations can lead to deforestation. The majority of Malaysian plantations are oil palm, with secondary crops such as rubber, fruit, and timber.

Plantation Breakdown by state

Outside of oil palm, northern states such as Kelantan and Kedah have the highest proportion of rubber plantations. Smaller states such as Perlis and Negeri Sembilan produce more fruit, while Sabah and Sarawak have more timber plantations. Hover over the bars to see the actual values.
