Which countries have the most forest?

Forests account for a little over one-third (38%) of habitable land area on earth. This consists of around 26% of total (both habitable and uninhabitable) land area, which varies significantly between countries.

Malaysia and Australia serve as intriguing case studies for deforestation comparisons due to their distinct geographical, economic, and environmental characteristics. Malaysia faces deforestation challenges driven by palm oil production and agriculture, with global implications for biodiversity and climate. Meanwhile, Australia grapples with land clearing for agriculture and mining, affecting its unique ecosystems and indigenous communities. By examining these two nations, we can explore the diverse drivers, environmental impacts, policy responses, and international ramifications of deforestation, shedding light on strategies for sustainable land use and conservation in different contexts.

Malaysia

Palm oil and logging

Malaysia is one of the world's largest producers and exporters of palm oil, a versatile and highly profitable crop used in various products. Forests have been cleared to make way for plant palm oil plantations. Malaysia also has a long history of timber production, and this industry has contributed to deforestation.

States like Johor, Pahang, and Sarawak have a high rate of deforestation primarily due to companies harvesting these natural resources.

Urbanization

Malaysia has experienced rapid urbanization, which can lead to deforestation as land is used for residential and commercial purposes.

Anti-logging Laws

Logging contributes to carbon emissions. Trees store carbon dioxide (CO2), a greenhouse gas, as they grow. When forests are cleared through logging or land conversion, the stored carbon is released into the atmosphere, contributing to climate change.

The ordinance mentioned in the chart aims to deter illegal logging and forest encroachment by imposing higher fines and stricter penalties for these activities. This (alongside other factors) had a clear effect on carbon emissions after the law was passed in 2015.
